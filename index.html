What are lattes?

Well, good question. By classic definition, a latte is an espresso-based drink made with a few shots of espresso and a large amount of steamed milk -- it has a thin layer of froth at the top. This creates a creamier drink than a capuccino or a flat-white, other espresso-based drinks. Delicious! Different additives also create such different flavor profiles. A Spanish Latte involves dissolving condensed milk with the espresso before adding the milk, adding a light sweetness to the latte. Stunning.

The term café latte goes back centuries and originates from Italy. However, modern espresso-based lattes originate from the U.S. where they gained major popularity from coffeehouses such as the ones in Seattle. In the new day and age, many cafes and drink connosieurs have started creating different renditions of the classic latte with non-espresso based concentrates. In drink-specialty shops, you can easily find lattes such as matcha lattes, hojicha lattes, earl grey, etc!
