My Favorite Lattes

Boston has such a vibrant coffee scene that it’s hard not to fall in love with the city’s café culture. We have North End's Italian roasters, the numerous Asian-inspired cafes sprinkled throughout Cambridge and Boston, the clean simple coffee shops like George Howell, and our favorite chain roasters like Caffe Nero. I often spend my weekends exploring new spots, catching up on work, or just relaxing with a good cup of coffee.

Ogawa Coffee, located in Downtown Boston, is a Japanese-style café known for its precision and artistry. Their matcha latte is my go-to — smooth, earthy, and beautifully whisked to perfection. During spring, they release a seasonal rose matcha latte that is lightly floral and not too sweet, perfect for warmer days.

In Somerville, Diesel Café has a much more local, laid-back energy. The space feels industrial yet cozy, and their Spanish latte is one of the best I’ve ever had. The condensed milk adds a light sweetness that balances perfectly with the espresso.

Below are some more of my favorite places that have left a lasting impression on me (taken directly from my Beli account).

Rating: 10

Phin Coffee

My bae. I don't think there's anything I've tried from this place that I haven't liked. - Cafe Phin - Matcha Snow - Coconut Coffee- Ube Matcha Latte (okay, lowkey this was just okay but it's always sold out whenever I go there these days so I'm not affected by it.) - Hojicha Latte I want to try the Boston Cream Latte + Golden Matcha Latte still.

Rating: 9.8

Phinista Cafe

Phinista is just such a classic. It's conveniently located for my purposes, and their drinks always hit. I really love their Iced Saigonese.

Rating: 9.6

The Sipping Room By Breeze

This is just such a cute little shop and it's the type of shop I aspire to open when I'm older. The drinks always taste super good and aren't overbearingly sweet. Admittedly, it's a little expensive but I always feel super at peace while here.

Rating: 8.9

Lucy and Jane Matcha

Omg this is so good! This is easily one of the best nonsweetened/lightly-sweetened matchas I've had. Although I usually prefer a less umami flavor, this one hits really nicely. I can taste the oat milk but it doesn't overpower the matcha. The foam added a nice creaminess to the drink. I would love to come again if it didn't slightly tank my wallet and if it were a little closer.

Rating: 8.2

bom dough coffee & dough

The location itself is really cute -- deceptively large. There's like three separate spaces that I think they rented out and connected. Their vibes overall are very comfy with ikea/MCM combo. They have these gorgerous ice glass walls on one side that i love. I got a guava matcha latte. So good!! The cheese bread is super warm and soft + the guava matcha isn't too sweet even with the syrup. The matcha flavor admittedly isnt' TOO strong, but at the price point I think it's pretty good.
